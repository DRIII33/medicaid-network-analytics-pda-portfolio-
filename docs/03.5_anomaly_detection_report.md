Anomaly Detection Findings Summary
1. Provider Claim Volume Outliers (99th Percentile Rule)
Four providers were identified as having an unusually high number of claims, exceeding the 99th percentile of all providers. These are:
PRV0147: 59 claims
PRV0229: 59 claims
PRV0207: 58 claims
PRV0177: 56 claims
These providers warrant further investigation to determine if their high volume is due to legitimate high patient load or potential anomalous billing practices.
2. Z-score Based Outlier Detection (for Claim Amount)
No individual claims were identified as having an amount_paid more than 3 standard deviations away from the mean. This suggests that while there might be variations in claim amounts, there are no extreme outliers that would immediately indicate highly unusual individual claim values.
3. Duplicate Member ID Use Across Providers
A significant finding was the identification of 748 member-week pairs where a single member had claims submitted by multiple different providers within the same week.
This is a potential red flag for issues such as duplicate billing, coordinated fraud, or system errors in member identification across different providers. Further investigation into these specific member-week pairs and associated providers is recommended.
4. Simple Clustering for Provider Claim Pattern Anomalies
KMeans clustering was applied to group providers based on their total claim volume and average amount paid. This analysis helps categorize providers into different behavioral patterns.
The clustering revealed different segments, which can be further analyzed to identify clusters that might represent higher risk (e.g., a cluster with a low number of claims but exceptionally high average claim amounts, or vice-versa).
The cluster assignments for each provider have been saved to provider_anomaly_scores.csv for detailed exploration.

Total Providers Analyzed: 500

This anomaly detection provides actionable insights for further investigation into potential payment integrity issues and unusual provider behavior within the Medicaid network.
